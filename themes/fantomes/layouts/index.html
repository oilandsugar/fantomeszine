1st Edition: Summer 2021
I initally came to this remote place to see Aleister Crowley's old residence - where he suposedly held a unfinished dark ritual - but now this little cemetery on the other side of the road was now calling me. I propped myself up on the broken tombstone leaning against the wall of the (only) small mausoleum, trying to peak inside through the broken window. I noticed that the black painted wood of the window frame was covered in grafiti, from people's names to the iconic "666" probably left there by another occultism nerd before me, fitting. The interior did not disapoint: obscure sigils carved onto the stone mantelpiece, remants of burned out candles on the floor and a hazy mist emanating from the floor. I quickly snapped this picture without even checking how it turned out, hopped down my makeshift step and walked away a little bit faster than I like to admit. I had forgotten how physical ghosts can be.
